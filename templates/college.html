Can I use my Permanent Life Insurance to finance kids' college?

Yes! Permanent life insurance is a type of policy that has a cash value component that grows tax-deferred over time. You can use this cash value to help pay for college expenses by borrowing against it or withdrawing from it. However, there are some pros and cons of using permanent life insurance for college funding.

Some pros are:

  1. Flexibility: You can use the cash value for any purpose, not just education-related expenses. You can also choose how much and when to withdraw or borrow from it
  2. Tax benefits: The cash value grows tax-deferred and the withdrawals or loans are usually tax-free, as long as they donâ€™t exceed the premiums paid. You may also qualify for a tax deduction on the interest paid on the policy loans.
  3. Financial aid: The cash value of a permanent life insurance policy is not considered an asset when applying for federal financial aid, unlike a 529 plan. This may increase your eligibility for grants and scholarships.

The cons include:

  1. Cost: Permanent life insurance policies are typically more expensive than term life policies or 529 plans. You have to pay for the insurance coverage, the fees, and the commissions. This may reduce the amount of money available for college savings.
  2. Risk: If you withdraw or borrow too much from the cash value, you may reduce the death benefit or lapse the policy. This may leave your beneficiaries without adequate protection or trigger a tax liability.
  3. Returns: The returns on the cash value may vary depending on the type of policy.
